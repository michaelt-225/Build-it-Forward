Build it Forward

Building Community, One Brick at a Time.

I have been a avid builder using LEGO bricks for as long as I could remember. They taught me patience, resourcefulness, and gave me the ability to rebuild when something wasn’t working. If LEGOs had taught me all that I knew they could teach others that too. I wanted kids—especially those who couldn’t afford LEGO sets—to have that too. It was time to give to the world the magic that LEGOs had bestowed upon me.

Starting Small

I started small. Posting flyers around town asking for donations, I focused on repurposing LEGO bricks that were collecting dust in the attics of families that no longer had a use for them. To my surprise, the response was overwhelming. Before long, my garage looked like a shipping warehouse, and I placed collection bins in public spaces to streamline this process.

Building Together

But something was missing—the interactivity and play that I love most about LEGO. I began hosting community events at which kids built their own designs: a mad scientist’s lab, a supersonic airplane, even a kid’s pet cat. I’d photograph each one, write step-by-step instructions, and repackage the disassembled LEGOs in a box. That way, the next child didn’t get random bricks, but a full set born from another kid’s imagination.

Looking Ahead

When I see scattered LEGO bricks across my room and feel the familiar studs pressed against my fingertips, my mind is at home. Even the smallest pieces can create something extraordinary—LEGOs have taught me that. And though my own foundation is still growing, brick-by-brick, I will carry that same patience and persistence into the larger communities I hope to build in the years ahead.
